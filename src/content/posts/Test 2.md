There is a difference between an AI product and a product with intelligence in the right place.

The first category often tries to make the model itself feel like the entire experience. The second starts with a clearer question: where does computation actually reduce friction, create leverage, or improve judgment?

That distinction matters.

Good AI integration is often quiet. It helps summarize without flattening nuance. It suggests without pretending to know everything. It makes workflows shorter or clearer instead of forcing a user to orbit around a model-shaped interface.

The challenge is not just technical. It is editorial. Someone has to decide what deserves amplification, what deserves omission, and what kind of behavior the product should normalize.

When that restraint is missing, intelligence becomes noise.
